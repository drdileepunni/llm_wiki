# Rituximab

## Overview
Chimeric monoclonal antibody against CD20, a B-cell surface antigen. Currently the preferred induction agent for [[ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV)]] including [[MPO-ANCA Vasculitis]].

## Mechanism
B-cell depletion via antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity and direct induction of apoptosis.

## Clinical Use in AAV
- **Status**: Now first-line for induction of remission
- **Evidence**: Non-inferior to [[Cyclophosphamide]] per [[RAVE Trial]]
- **Advantages over cyclophosphamide**:
  - Fewer long-term toxicity concerns
  - Preserved fertility
  - Better tolerability profile

## Dosing

### Induction
1000 mg IV on days 0 and 14 (combined with high-dose glucocorticoids)

### Maintenance (per Chalkia & Jayne 2024)
**Fixed-interval strategy**: 500 mg IV every 6 months for 24-48 months
- Superior to [[Azathioprine]] and [[Methotrexate]] for maintaining remission
- Permits glucocorticoid discontinuation within 6 months
- Reduces relapse rates compared to traditional immunosuppressants
