# Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)

Formerly known as Wegener granulomatosis.

## Definition
A systemic necrotizing vasculitis of small and medium vessels characterized by PR3-ANCA positivity, necrotizing granulomatous inflammation, and glomerulonephritis.

## ANCA Association
- **ANCA type**: PR3-ANCA (proteinase 3) in ~90% of cases
- **c-ANCA pattern** on immunofluorescence

## Clinical Features
- **Respiratory involvement**: Upper and lower respiratory tract involvement (distinguishes from [[Microscopic Polyangiitis (MPA)]])
  - Pulmonary nodules (common, with cavitation)
  - Sinusitis
  - Otitis media
- **Renal**: Glomerulonephritis (rapidly progressive)
- **Systemic**: Constitutional symptoms, arthralgia, skin manifestations
- **CNS**: Cranial nerve involvement, vasculitis

## Treatment (per Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### Induction
**First-line**: Glucocorticoids + [[Rituximab]] (preferred over [[Cyclophosphamide]])
- PEXIVAS rapid steroid taper reduces infection risk
- Rituximab 500 mg IV on days 0, 7, 14, 21

### Maintenance
**Fixed-interval rituximab**: 500 mg IV every 6 months for 24-48 months
- Superior to [[Azathioprine]] or [[Methotrexate]]
- Permits glucocorticoid discontinuation within 6 months

### Complement-Targeted Therapy
[[Avacopan]] (C5a antagonist) superior to standard steroid tapering when added to rituximab/cyclophosphamide

## Prognosis
- Improved with modern treatment (rituximab-based)
- Early and rapid treatment initiation critical
- Relapse rates lower with rituximab maintenance vs traditional ISA
