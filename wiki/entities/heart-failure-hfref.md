# Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)

## Definition

Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF) is defined as clinical heart failure with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≤40%. It represents systolic dysfunction where the heart cannot pump sufficient blood to meet the body's metabolic demands.

## Classification by Ejection Fraction

- **HFrEF:** LVEF ≤40%
- **HFmrEF (mildly reduced):** LVEF 41-49%
- **HFpEF (preserved):** LVEF ≥50%
- **HFimpEF (improved):** Previously ≤40%, now >40%

## Etiology

### Most Common Causes
1. **Ischemic cardiomyopathy** — coronary artery disease, prior myocardial infarction (most common in developed countries)
2. **Non-ischemic dilated cardiomyopathy**
   - Idiopathic
   - Viral myocarditis
   - Alcohol-related
   - Chemotherapy-induced (anthracyclines, trastuzumab)
   - Peripartum cardiomyopathy
3. **Chronic hypertension**
4. **Valvular heart disease** — severe mitral regurgitation or aortic regurgitation
5. **Genetic/familial cardiomyopathies**
6. **Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy**

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Dyspnea (exertional, at rest, orthopnea, PND)
- Fatigue, weakness
- Reduced exercise tolerance
- Fluid retention (edema, weight gain)
- Abdominal bloating

### Physical Examination
- Elevated JVP
- Displaced apical impulse (LV enlargement)
- S3 gallop (hallmark finding)
- Pulmonary crackles
- Peripheral edema
- Hepatomegaly
- Cool extremities (low cardiac output)

### Functional Classification (NYHA)
- **Class I:** No limitation; ordinary activity does not cause symptoms
- **Class II:** Slight limitation; comfortable at rest, ordinary activity causes symptoms
- **Class III:** Marked limitation; comfortable at rest, less-than-ordinary activity causes symptoms
- **Class IV:** Severe limitation; symptoms at rest

## Diagnostic Evaluation

- **Echocardiography** — gold standard for measuring LVEF; assess chamber size, wall motion, valves
- **BNP or NT-proBNP** — elevated in volume overload and ventricular dysfunction
- **Chest X-ray** — cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema
- **EKG** — may show prior MI, LVH, bundle branch blocks, arrhythmias
- **Cardiac MRI** — if echo inadequate; can assess viability, infiltrative disease
- **Coronary angiography** — if ischemic etiology suspected
- **Labs:** CBC, CMP, TSH, lipid panel, consider iron studies

## Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)

See [[Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy]] for full details.

### Core Therapies (reduce mortality)

1. **ACE inhibitor or ARB** — if intolerant to ARNI
2. **ARNI (Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor)** — sacubitril/valsartan (preferred over ACE/ARB)
3. **Beta-blocker** — carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, or bisoprolol
4. **Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA)** — spironolactone or eplerenone
5. **SGLT2 inhibitor** — dapagliflozin or empagliflozin (regardless of diabetes status)

### Additional Therapies
- **Loop diuretic** — for volume management (does not reduce mortality but essential for symptom control)
- **Hydralazine + nitrate** — if ARNI/ACE/ARB contraindicated, or in African American patients as adjunct
- **Ivabradine** — if sinus rhythm with HR ≥70 despite beta-blocker
- **Vericiguat** — if recent HF hospitalization and elevated natriuretic peptides
- **Digoxin** — may reduce hospitalizations, does not reduce mortality

### Device Therapy
- **ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)** — if LVEF ≤35% despite 3+ months optimal medical therapy (primary prevention of sudden cardiac death)
- **CRT (Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy)** — if LVEF ≤35%, LBBB, QRS ≥150 ms, NYHA Class II-IV on optimal therapy

### Advanced Therapies
- **Mechanical circulatory support** (LVAD)
- **Cardiac transplantation**

## Case Evidence

### Davis 2026 ([[davis-2026-adhf-case]])

A 75-year-old with ischemic cardiomyopathy and HFrEF presented with acute decompensation:

**Baseline Status:**
- LVEF 25-30% (January 2026 echo)
- Ischemic etiology (prior MI 2019)
- Chronic comorbidities: Stage 3b CKD (baseline Cr 1.8), atrial fibrillation, diabetes, hypertension, OSA

**ADHF Admission (April 2026):**
- Precipitants: dietary indiscretion, medication non-adherence, possible CPAP non-compliance
- Severe volume overload: 12 lb weight gain, BNP 3,840 pg/mL
- Echo during admission showed further EF decline to 20-25%, moderate-to-severe functional MR

**GDMT Optimization Achieved During Hospitalization:**
- **Sacubitril/valsartan 24/26 mg BID** — initiated (ACE-i held ≥36 hours prior per guidelines)
- **Dapagliflozin 10 mg daily** — SGLT2i added
- **Metoprolol succinate 100 mg daily** — uptitrated from 50 mg
- **Spironolactone 25 mg daily** — continued
- **Furosemide 80 mg BID** — doubled from home dose of 40 mg daily

**Key Points:**
1. This case demonstrates aggressive GDMT optimization during hospitalization rather than deferring to outpatient follow-up
2. ARNI and SGLT2i were safely initiated despite concurrent cardiorenal syndrome (creatinine 2.0-2.4 mg/dL)
3. Patient discharged on 4 of 4 core GDMT pillars (ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
4. Advanced heart failure team referral placed given progressive disease despite optimal therapy

## Prognosis

- Highly variable depending on etiology, LVEF, GDMT adherence, comorbidities
- 5-year mortality: 50-75% if untreated or inadequately treated
- GDMT reduces mortality by 30-50% compared to placebo
- Recurrent hospitalizations predict worse outcomes
- Sudden cardiac death risk: 30-50% of mortality in HFrEF
