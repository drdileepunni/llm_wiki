# MPO-ANCA Vasculitis

Also known as [[Microscopic Polyangiitis (MPA)]].

## Definition
A systemic necrotizing vasculitis of small vessels characterized by ANCA antibodies against myeloperoxidase (MPO). Accounts for 40-50% of all ANCA-associated vasculitis cases.

## Clinical Presentation
- Glomerulonephritis (hallmark feature, rapid progression)
- Constitutional symptoms
- Arthralgia
- Pulmonary involvement (nodules are rare, unlike GPA)

## Treatment
### Induction
**First-line**: Rituximab (demonstrated non-inferiority to cyclophosphamide in [[RAVE Trial]])
**Alternative**: Cyclophosphamide for severe disease or rituximab contraindications

### Maintenance
- Azathioprine or mycophenolate mofetil
- Low-dose corticosteroids
- Therapy typically continued for 12-24 months post-remission

## Related Conditions
- [[ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV)]]
- [[Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)]] (contrast: GPA has pulmonary nodules)
