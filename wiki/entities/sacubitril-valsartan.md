# Sacubitril-valsartan

**Drug class:** Angiotensin receptor-neprilysin inhibitor (ARNI)

**Components:**
- Sacubitril — neprilysin inhibitor (increases natriuretic peptides)
- Valsartan — angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB)

**Primary indication:** Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction ([[HFrEF]])

---

## Use in Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

### Withholding During ADHF

Controversy exists regarding continuation vs. withholding of sacubitril-valsartan during acute decompensation.

---

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Clinical scenario:**
- Patient on home sacubitril-valsartan
- Presented with [[acute decompensated heart failure]]
- SBP 85 mmHg on arrival (haemodynamic instability)
- Creatinine 162 µmol/L, baseline 115 µmol/L (significant [[acute kidney injury]])
- eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m²

**Management decision:** Sacubitril-valsartan **held on admission**

**Rationale from treating team:**
- Continuing ARNI during active ADHF with AKI risks precipitating dangerous hypotension
- Risk of worsening renal perfusion
- "Sacubitril-valsartan should be withheld until the patient is euvolaemic, haemodynamically stable, and renal function is recovering"

**Re-introduction:** Day 7 at reduced dose, after:
- Euvolaemia achieved
- Haemodynamic stabilization
- Renal function recovering (creatinine improving from peak 174 µmol/L)

**Key principle:** Prioritize haemodynamic stability and renal perfusion during acute phase, then cautiously reintroduce neurohormonal blockade during recovery phase.

Source: [[Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case]]

---

## Contraindications and Cautions

Based on clinical experience:
- Hypotension (SBP <90-95 mmHg)
- Significant acute kidney injury
- Haemodynamic instability during ADHF
