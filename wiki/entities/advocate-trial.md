# ADVOCATE Trial

## Overview
Pivotal randomized controlled trial establishing the efficacy of [[Avacopan]] (C5a receptor antagonist) in ANCA-associated vasculitis when combined with standard immunosuppression.

## Trial Design
Compared:
- **Avacopan** + standard glucocorticoid/rituximab(or cyclophosphamide) induction
- **Standard glucocorticoid taper** + standard immunosuppression (control)

## Key Findings (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### Superiority to Standard Steroid Taper
- **Primary outcome**: Avacopan regimen superior to standard glucocorticoid tapering
- **Faster proteinuria reduction** in kidney disease
- **Better kidney recovery** — higher proportion achieving improvement in kidney function

### Steroid-Sparing Benefit
- Permits more rapid glucocorticoid discontinuation
- Reduces cumulative steroid exposure
- Maintains remission induction efficacy

### Clinical Indication
- Approved for [[ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV)]] induction therapy in combination with rituximab or cyclophosphamide
- Particularly strong evidence in [[Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)]] and [[Microscopic Polyangiitis (MPA)]]
- Limited data in [[Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)]]

## Treatment Algorithm Impact

### Modern AAV Induction (Post-ADVOCATE)
1. High-dose glucocorticoids (rapid taper)
2. [[Rituximab]] (B-cell targeted, standard)
3. **[[Avacopan]]** (C5a antagonist, now standard of care)

### "Dual-hit" Strategy
- Rituximab targets ANCA production
- Avacopan targets complement-mediated tissue damage
- Synergistic efficacy with steroid-sparing approach
