# PEXIVAS Trial

## Overview
Landmark trial establishing the safety and efficacy of rapid glucocorticoid tapering in ANCA-associated vasculitis, and the role of plasma exchange in severe disease.

## Trial Design
Randomized controlled trial comparing:
- **Glucocorticoid schedule**: Rapid taper vs standard taper
- **Plasma exchange**: Addition of plasma exchange vs standard treatment

## Key Findings (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### Rapid Glucocorticoid Tapering
- **Safety**: Rapid taper (PEXIVAS schedule) **reduces serious infections** without loss of remission efficacy
- **Efficacy maintained**: Non-inferior to standard steroid taper for achieving remission
- **Clinical impact**: Demonstrates that prolonged high-dose steroids unnecessary

### Plasma Exchange Benefit
- Indicated when **serum creatinine >300 µmol/L** (severe kidney dysfunction)
- Beneficial in **rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis**
- Mechanism: Clears circulating ANCA and circulating immune complexes

## Clinical Implications

### Modern Induction Regimen
Based on PEXIVAS findings:
1. High-dose glucocorticoids (pulse IV methylprednisolone)
2. Rapid oral steroid taper (per PEXIVAS schedule)
3. [[Rituximab]] or [[Cyclophosphamide]] for immunosuppression
4. Plasma exchange for severe kidney disease (Cr >300 µmol/L)

### Infection Prevention
- Rapid steroid reduction critically important for avoiding serious infections
- Allows rituximab to work without high-dose steroid immunosuppression
- Improves long-term safety profile

## Relationship to Modern Agents

### With Avacopan
- [[Avacopan]] further enables rapid steroid discontinuation
- "PEXIVAS-inspired" rapid taper + avacopan achieves best steroid-sparing outcomes
