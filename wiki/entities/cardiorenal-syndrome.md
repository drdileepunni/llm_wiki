# Cardiorenal Syndrome

**Definition:** Pathophysiologic disorder of the heart and kidneys whereby acute or chronic dysfunction in one organ induces acute or chronic dysfunction in the other.

**Key concept:** Bidirectional interaction between cardiac and renal function — heart failure worsens kidney function, kidney dysfunction worsens heart failure.

---

## Clinical Manifestations

### In Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

- Worsening renal function during diuresis (rise in creatinine, fall in eGFR)
- Reduced urine output despite adequate diuretic dosing
- Conflict between decongestion goals and renal preservation
- Risk of precipitating dialysis-dependent renal failure with aggressive diuresis

---

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Clinical presentation:**
- Known [[HFrEF]] (EF 28%) presenting with [[acute decompensated heart failure]]
- Baseline creatinine 115 µmol/L → 162 µmol/L on presentation
- eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m² (advanced [[chronic kidney disease]])
- Acute-on-chronic kidney injury in context of cardiac decompensation

**Management approach:**

The treating team interpreted worsening renal function during diuresis as **true cardiorenal syndrome requiring diuretic dose reduction**, not escalation:

- Conservative [[furosemide]] dosing: 80 mg IV BD (bolus, not infusion)
- Lower urine output targets: >100 mL/hour
- Creatinine monitored daily
- When creatinine rose from 162 → 174 µmol/L, strategy was **maintained** (not escalated)
- [[Tolvaptan]] added as aquaretic adjunct rather than increasing loop diuretic dose

**Outcome:**
- Creatinine peaked at 174 µmol/L then improved to 130 µmol/L at discharge
- Avoided dialysis-dependent renal failure
- Successful decongestion (BNP 3,100 → 780 pg/mL)
- No 30-day readmission

**Clinical principle from source:**
> "Worsening renal function during diuresis in this context represents true cardiorenal syndrome and warrants dose adjustment, not continuation of aggressive diuresis."

> "The primary treatment target in high-risk cardiorenal patients is preservation of renal function alongside decongestion — not decongestion at any cost."

Source: [[Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case]]

---

## Management Principles

Based on clinical evidence:

1. **In advanced CKD (eGFR <35), prioritize renal preservation** alongside decongestion
2. **Rising creatinine during diuresis may signal need to reduce diuretic intensity**, not increase it
3. **Consider aquaretic adjuncts** ([[tolvaptan]]) rather than loop diuretic escalation
4. **Slower decongestion with renal preservation may be preferable** to rapid decongestion with permanent renal injury
5. **Withhold renin-angiotensin system inhibitors** ([[sacubitril-valsartan]]) during acute phase if haemodynamic instability or significant AKI present
