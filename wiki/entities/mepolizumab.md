# Mepolizumab

## Overview
Humanized monoclonal antibody against interleukin-5 (IL-5). Approved for [[Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)]] and other eosinophil-driven diseases.

## Mechanism
Blocks IL-5 signaling, preventing eosinophil development, recruitment, and activation. First-in-class targeted therapy for eosinophilic inflammation in EGPA.

## Clinical Efficacy in EGPA (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### Key Findings
- **Permits glucocorticoid reduction** — enables steroid-sparing outcomes
- **Improves remission rates** — better disease control vs traditional ISA
- **Reduces relapse frequency** — sustained benefit maintained beyond 1 year
- **Well-tolerated** — favorable safety profile

### Clinical Benefit
Transforms [[Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)]] from a disease requiring prolonged high-dose glucocorticoids to one where remission can be achieved and maintained with minimal steroid exposure.

## Dosing
250 mg (3.0 mL) subcutaneous injection monthly or 300 mg IV based on formulation and indication

## Position in EGPA Treatment Algorithm

### Induction Phase
- Used adjunctively with glucocorticoids (± rituximab for severe disease)

### Maintenance Phase
- Often becomes sole immunosuppressive agent
- Permits glucocorticoid discontinuation
- Reduces cumulative glucocorticoid toxicity

## Advantages
- **Targeted mechanism** — addresses eosinophilic pathophysiology
- **Glucocorticoid-sparing** — significant reduction in long-term steroid burden
- **Eosinophil-reduction** — normalizes peripheral eosinophil counts
- **Preventive** — prevents relapse better than traditional ISA

## Disease-Specific Use
**EGPA-selective**: IL-5 inhibition is disease-specific and transformative for EGPA
**GPA/MPA**: Minimal direct role (not IL-5 driven)

## Comparison with [[Rituximab]]
- **Rituximab**: B-cell targeted, used in GPA/MPA and severe EGPA
- **Mepolizumab**: IL-5 targeted, preferred for EGPA (unless severe or refractory)

## Related Biologic
[[Reslizumab]] — anti-IL-5 receptor (alternative IL-5 pathway inhibition)
