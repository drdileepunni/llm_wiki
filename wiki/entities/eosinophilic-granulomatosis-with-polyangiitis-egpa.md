# Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)

Formerly known as Churg-Strauss syndrome.

## Definition
A systemic necrotizing vasculitis characterized by eosinophil-rich granulomatous inflammation, asthma, and often ANCA positivity.

## Key Distinguishing Features
- **Asthma** — usually precedes vasculitis (often severe)
- **Eosinophilia** — prominent (blood and tissue)
- **ANCA status** — ANCA-positive in ~40-60% of cases (when positive: MPO-ANCA more common than PR3-ANCA)
- **Organ involvement**: Pulmonary, cardiac, neurological, cutaneous more common than renal

## Clinical Presentation

### Phase 1: Prodromal Asthma Phase
- Asthma (can appear years before vasculitis)
- Allergies, sinusitis

### Phase 2: Eosinophilic Phase
- Hypereosinophilia (blood and tissue)
- Pulmonary or GI involvement

### Phase 3: Vasculitic Phase
- Necrotizing vasculitis
- Multi-organ involvement
- Systemic symptoms

## Treatment (per Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### Induction
**Glucocorticoids** as first-line for mild-moderate disease
**Glucocorticoids + immunosuppression** (rituximab or cyclophosphamide) for severe disease

### Maintenance with IL-5 Inhibition
**[[Mepolizumab]] (anti-IL-5 antibody)** — Key innovation for EGPA:
- Permits glucocorticoid reduction
- Improves remission rates
- Reduces relapse
- Benefit maintained beyond 1 year

### Alternative Biologic
**[[Reslizumab]]** — anti-IL-5 receptor (emerging option)

## Prognosis
- Better overall prognosis than GPA/MPA when cardiac involvement absent
- Cardiac involvement (myocarditis, cardiomyopathy) carries higher mortality risk
- Mepolizumab has significantly improved outcomes

## Controversy: ANCA-Negative EGPA
- ~40-60% are ANCA-negative
- Treatment approach similar to ANCA-positive but some data suggest different pathophysiology
- May benefit from different biomarker-guided strategies (IL-5 targeting universally relevant)
