# Avacopan

## Overview
A selective C5a receptor antagonist (oral small molecule) approved for [[ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV)]] including [[Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)]] and [[Microscopic Polyangiitis (MPA)]].

## Mechanism
Blocks complement component C5a (complement C5a) binding to C5aR (complement receptor 1), preventing recruitment and activation of neutrophils. Acts downstream of B-cell pathology, complementing [[B-cell Depletion]] strategies.

## Clinical Efficacy (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

### ADVOCATE Trial Evidence
- **Superior to standard glucocorticoid taper** when combined with [[Rituximab]] or [[Cyclophosphamide]]
- **Faster proteinuria reduction** in kidney disease
- **Improved kidney recovery** — higher proportion achieving kidney function improvement
- **Steroid-sparing effect** — permits more rapid GC reduction/discontinuation

### Indication
- AAV induction therapy in combination with standard immunosuppression
- Particularly effective in GPA/MPA (benefit established)
- Limited data in [[Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)]]

## Dosing
300 mg orally twice daily (typically given in induction phase)

## Advantages over Traditional Steroid Taper
- Avoids prolonged glucocorticoid exposure
- Reduces infection and metabolic complications
- Maintains remission induction efficacy

## Side Effects
- Generally well-tolerated
- Neisseria meningitidis infection risk (vaccination required)
- Meningococcal prophylaxis recommended

## Position in Treatment Algorithm
Used in combination with:
- [[Rituximab]] (preferred combination)
- [[Cyclophosphamide]] (alternative)
- High-dose glucocorticoids (rapidly tapered)
