# Furosemide

**Drug class:** Loop diuretic

**Mechanism:** Inhibits Na-K-2Cl cotransporter in thick ascending limb of loop of Henle, promoting natriuresis and diuresis

**Primary indications:** Fluid overload, heart failure, pulmonary edema, chronic kidney disease with volume overload

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Clinical scenario:**
- [[Acute decompensated heart failure]]
- eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m² (advanced [[chronic kidney disease]])
- Creatinine 162 µmol/L on presentation (baseline 115 µmol/L)
- Significant acute-on-chronic kidney injury

**Dosing strategy:** Conservative, stepped approach
- **Dose:** Furosemide 80 mg IV BD (bolus, not infusion)
- **Rationale:** High-dose loop diuretic infusions in context of significant CKD carry unacceptable risk of further AKI, potentially precipitating dialysis-dependent renal failure
- **Urine output target:** >100 mL/hour (less aggressive than standard targets)
- **Monitoring:** Daily electrolytes and creatinine

**Outcome:**
- Net fluid removal 2.5 L by Day 2 (slower than aggressive strategies)
- Creatinine rose to 174 µmol/L (peak Day 2-3) before plateauing and falling by Day 4
- Renal function preserved — discharge creatinine 130 µmol/L

**Clinical conclusion from treating team:**
- "Conservative diuresis preserves renal function in high-risk CKD patients"
- "Forcing rapid decongestion risks permanent renal injury that outweighs benefit of faster fluid removal"
- "Worsening renal function during diuresis in this context represents true [[cardiorenal syndrome]] and warrants dose adjustment, not continuation of aggressive diuresis"

**Adjunct used:** [[Tolvaptan]] 15 mg daily added Day 3 when urine output insufficient, avoiding further furosemide escalation

## Nephrotoxicity

Loop diuretics can cause or worsen acute kidney injury, particularly:
- High doses
- Continuous infusions in vulnerable patients
- Patients with pre-existing chronic kidney disease
- Context of haemodynamic instability or cardiorenal syndrome

See: [[Loop diuretic nephrotoxicity]]
