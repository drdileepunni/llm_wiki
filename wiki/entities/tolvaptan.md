# Tolvaptan

**Drug class:** Vasopressin V2-receptor antagonist (aquaretic)

**Mechanism:** Blocks vasopressin V2 receptors in renal collecting duct, promoting free water excretion without electrolyte loss (aquaresis)

---

## Clinical Use

### Heart Failure

Tolvaptan is used as an adjunct to loop diuretics in patients with acute decompensated heart failure, particularly when:
- Inadequate response to loop diuretics alone
- Concern for nephrotoxicity with further loop diuretic escalation
- Hyponatremia present
- Advanced chronic kidney disease limits diuretic options

---

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Dose:** 15 mg orally daily

**Indication:** Added on Day 3 of admission when urine output was deemed insufficient despite adequate loop diuretic dosing ([[furosemide]] 80 mg IV BD) in patient with eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m²

**Rationale:** The aquaretic mechanism (free water excretion without electrolyte loss) allowed additional fluid removal without further nephrotoxic loop diuretic escalation

**Outcome:**
- Additional decongestion achieved
- Sodium remained stable (no electrolyte disturbance)
- No additional renal injury attributed to tolvaptan

**Clinical conclusion from treating team:** "Tolvaptan is a valuable adjunct in patients with CKD and inadequate response to loop diuretics, enabling decongestion while limiting renal risk."
