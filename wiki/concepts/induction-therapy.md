# Induction Therapy

## Definition
The initial phase of immunosuppressive treatment aimed at rapidly achieving disease remission in systemic vasculitis.

## In ANCA-Associated Vasculitis
The goal is to suppress disease activity quickly to prevent organ damage, particularly irreversible renal scarring.

### Current Standards (per Chalkia & Jayne 2024, EULAR 2022)

#### First-Line Regimen
**Glucocorticoids + [[Rituximab]]**
- Rituximab: 1000 mg IV on days 0 and 14
- Corticosteroids: High-dose IV methylprednisolone followed by rapid oral taper (PEXIVAS schedule)
- **Advantage**: Non-inferior to cyclophosphamide with better long-term tolerability

#### Alternative for Severe Disease
**[[Cyclophosphamide]]** (when rituximab contraindicated or for very severe presentations)

#### Adjunctive Complement-Targeted Therapy
**[[Avacopan]]** (C5a antagonist) superior to standard steroid tapering
- Faster proteinuria reduction
- Better kidney function recovery
- Permits more rapid glucocorticoid discontinuation

#### Plasma Exchange
Indicated when serum creatinine >300 µmol/L or rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis
- Clears circulating ANCA and immune complexes
- Combination with rituximab/cyclophosphamide improves kidney outcomes

### Duration
Typically 3-6 months until remission achieved
