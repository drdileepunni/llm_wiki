# Aquaretic Therapy

**Definition:** The use of agents that promote excretion of free water (aquaresis) without significant electrolyte loss, as distinguished from traditional diuretics that promote natriuresis (sodium and water excretion together).

**Mechanism:** Blockade of vasopressin V2 receptors in the renal collecting duct, preventing water reabsorption while leaving electrolyte handling largely intact.

**Primary agent:** [[Tolvaptan]] (vasopressin V2-receptor antagonist)

---

## Clinical Rationale

### Advantages over Loop Diuretics

1. **No additional nephrotoxicity** — does not act on renal tubules in the same way as loop diuretics
2. **No electrolyte disturbance** — free water excretion without sodium, potassium, or chloride loss
3. **Complementary mechanism** — can be added to loop diuretics for additional decongestion without escalating loop diuretic dose
4. **Particularly useful in:**
   - Hyponatremia
   - Advanced chronic kidney disease where loop diuretic escalation risks AKI
   - Inadequate response to loop diuretics alone ("diuretic resistance")

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Clinical context:**
- Patient with eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m² and [[acute decompensated heart failure]]
- On [[furosemide]] 80 mg IV BD with insufficient urine output by Day 3
- Concern that escalating loop diuretic dose would worsen [[acute kidney injury]]

**Intervention:**
- [[Tolvaptan]] 15 mg PO daily added Day 3

**Mechanism exploited:**
- Free water excretion without electrolyte loss
- Additional fluid removal without further nephrotoxic loop diuretic escalation

**Outcome:**
- Additional decongestion achieved
- Sodium remained stable (no electrolyte disturbance)
- No additional renal injury attributed to tolvaptan
- Creatinine peaked at 174 µmol/L then improved to 130 µmol/L at discharge

**Clinical conclusion from treating team:**
> "Tolvaptan is a valuable adjunct in patients with CKD and inadequate response to loop diuretics, enabling decongestion while limiting renal risk."

## Clinical Applications

### Heart Failure with Advanced CKD

In the context of [[cardiorenal syndrome]], aquaretic therapy allows:
- Continued decongestion when loop diuretic dose is maximized or limited by renal concerns
- Avoidance of electrolyte derangement in patients on multiple medications affecting electrolytes
- Water removal in volume-overloaded patients with hyponatremia (corrects dilutional hyponatremia)

### Hyponatremia

Aquaretics selectively remove free water, raising serum sodium concentration — useful in:
- SIADH (syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone secretion)
- Heart failure with dilutional hyponatremia
- Cirrhosis with hyponatremia

---

## Limitations and Cautions

- **Cost** — tolvaptan is expensive compared to loop diuretics
- **Hepatotoxicity risk** — FDA black box warning for potential liver injury (primarily with chronic use in ADPKD)
- **Requires free water intake restriction** — must be carefully monitored to avoid hypernatremia
- **Not a replacement for loop diuretics** — aquaretics remove water but not sodium; volume overload in heart failure includes both

---

## Contrast with Natriuretic Diuretics

| Feature | Aquaretics (e.g. tolvaptan) | Loop diuretics (e.g. furosemide) |
|---------|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Water excretion | Yes (primary effect) | Yes (secondary to sodium loss) |
| Sodium excretion | Minimal | Yes (primary effect) |
| Electrolyte disturbance | Minimal (risk of hypernatremia if excessive) | Hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, metabolic alkalosis |
| Nephrotoxicity | Minimal | Yes (dose-dependent) |
| Effect on dilutional hyponatremia | Corrects it | May worsen it |
| Use in CKD | Relatively safe | Requires dose escalation, higher AKI risk |
