# Complement Inhibition

## Definition
Blocking components of the complement cascade (innate immune system) to prevent neutrophil activation and tissue inflammation in ANCA-associated vasculitis.

## Complement Pathophysiology in AAV

### Role of C5a
- C5a is a potent neutrophil chemoattractant and activator
- Generated downstream of both classical and alternative complement pathways
- Drives recruitment of neutrophils to vessel walls → necrotizing inflammation

### Link to ANCA
- ANCA-primed neutrophils produce complement-activating agents
- ANCA + complement activation creates "two-hit" mechanism of tissue damage
- Blocking complement acts synergistically with B-cell depletion ([[Rituximab]])

## Complement Inhibition in AAV Treatment

### C5a Receptor Antagonism: [[Avacopan]]

**Mechanism**: Selective blockade of C5a receptor (C5aR), preventing neutrophil recruitment

**Clinical Evidence** (ADVOCATE Trial):
- Superior to standard glucocorticoid tapering
- When combined with [[Rituximab]] or [[Cyclophosphamide]]:
  - Faster proteinuria reduction
  - Better kidney function recovery
  - Permits more rapid glucocorticoid discontinuation

**Mechanism advantage**: Acts downstream of both classical and alternative pathways
- Doesn't block C5 cleavage entirely (preserving some immune function)
- Specifically targets the potent neutrophil activator (C5a)

### Other Complement Targets (Emerging)

**C5 inhibitors**: Eculizumab, pegcetacoplan (direct C5 complement inhibitors)
- Broader complement blockade
- More infections reported historically

**Factor D, alternative pathway inhibitors**: Research ongoing

## Strategic Use in AAV

### Induction Phase
- **Preferred regimen**: Rituximab (B-cell depletion) + Avacopan (complement inhibition)
- Dual-pathway blocking superior to single-agent approaches
- Steroid-sparing effect significant

### Maintenance Phase
- Less clear benefit; rituximab alone often sufficient
- May be considered in high-risk relapse patients

## Advantages of C5a Targeting over Alternative Approaches

| Feature | C5a antagonism (Avacopan) | C5 inhibition | Full complement blockade |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Target specificity | High (C5aR only) | Partial (blocks C5) | Broad |
| Immune function preserved | Better | Reduced | Reduced |
| Infection risk | Lower | Moderate | Higher |
| Meningococcal prophylaxis | Required | Required | Required |
| Nephrology outcomes | Superior | Not yet proven in AAV | Unknown |

## Mechanism Synergy with [[B-cell Depletion]]

- [[Rituximab]] targets B cells (source of ANCA autoantibodies)
- [[Avacopan]] targets downstream complement (tissue effector mechanism)
- Combined approach blocks both initiation AND amplification of inflammation

## Clinical Application
Modern AAV induction: "Dual-hit" strategy with rituximab + avacopan
