# Interleukin-5 Inhibition

## Definition
Targeted blockade of interleukin-5 (IL-5) signaling pathway to prevent eosinophil development, recruitment, and activation.

## IL-5 Pathophysiology in EGPA

### Role of IL-5
- Master cytokine for eosinophil development, growth, and survival
- Produced by Th2 lymphocytes (driven by allergic/asthmatic pathways)
- Drives eosinophil recruitment to tissues (particularly lungs, GI, heart)

### IL-5 in [[Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA)]]
- Central to eosinophilic infiltration phase
- Maintains eosinophilic vasculitis
- Responsible for eosinophil-mediated tissue damage

## Treatment Target: [[Mepolizumab]]

### Mechanism
Monoclonal antibody against IL-5, preventing eosinophil-driving signaling

### Clinical Impact (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

**Efficacy in EGPA**:
- Permits glucocorticoid reduction (steroid-sparing)
- Improves remission rates
- Reduces relapse frequency
- Benefit sustained >1 year

**Transformation of EGPA management**:
- Shifts from prolonged high-dose GC dependence
- Enables remission maintenance with minimal steroid exposure
- Provides sustained eosinophil control

### Dosing
- 250 mg SC monthly OR
- 300 mg IV based on formulation

## Disease-Specific Application

### EGPA (Disease-Defining)
- **Preferred maintenance agent** for eosinophil-driven disease
- Often becomes sole immunosuppressive after induction
- Can be used adjunctively with low-dose GC/ISA if needed

### GPA/MPA
- **No direct role** — these diseases not IL-5 driven
- Eosinophilia, if present, is secondary/reactive
- B-cell targeting (rituximab) more appropriate

## IL-5 Pathway Antagonists

### Direct IL-5 Antagonists
- **[[Mepolizumab]]** (anti-IL-5, most established)

### IL-5 Receptor Antagonists
- **[[Reslizumab]]** (anti-IL-5R, alternative pathway blockade)
- **Benralizumab** (anti-IL-5R alpha, complement-dependent eosinophil depletion)

All three modalities effective; mepolizumab most clinical trial data in AAV context.

## Advantages of IL-5 Targeting in EGPA

| Aspect | Mepolizumab-based approach | Traditional GC approach |
|--------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| Eosinophil control | Sustained | GC-dependent, rebound risk |
| Glucocorticoid requirement | Minimal | High-dose maintenance |
| Long-term toxicity | Lower | Higher (bone, infection, CVD) |
| Relapse prevention | Superior | Frequent relapses |
| Remission quality | Better | Fragile, GC-dependent |

## Strategic Use in AAV

### Induction Phase
- Adjunctive with glucocorticoids (± rituximab for severe disease)

### Maintenance Phase
- Becomes primary maintenance agent
- Often enables complete GC discontinuation
- Sustained monotherapy possible

## Contrast with [[B-cell Depletion]]

- [[Rituximab]]: Targets ANCA production (B cells) — effective for ANCA+ vasculitis
- **IL-5 inhibition**: Targets eosinophil pathway — effective for eosinophil-driven disease (EGPA)
- Complementary mechanisms for different AAV subtypes

## Mechanisms NOT Targeted by IL-5 Inhibition

- ANCA production (need rituximab)
- Neutrophil activation (complement drives in GPA/MPA)
- Granulomatous inflammation (in GPA)

→ IL-5 selective for eosinophilic pathophysiology
