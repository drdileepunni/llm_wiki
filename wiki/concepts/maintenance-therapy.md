# Maintenance Therapy

## Definition
The long-term phase of immunosuppressive treatment following [[Induction Therapy]], aimed at preserving remission and preventing relapse.

## Paradigm Shift in AAV Maintenance

### Legacy Strategy (Azathioprine/Methotrexate)
- Unclear benefit vs harmful in preventing relapse
- Prolonged glucocorticoid requirements (high-dose maintenance steroids)
- Poor tolerability over years

### Modern Strategy: Fixed-Interval Rituximab (Chalkia & Jayne 2024)

**Superior to traditional immunosuppressants**:
- [[Rituximab]] 500 mg IV every 6 months for 24-48 months
- **Outcome**: Permits glucocorticoid discontinuation within 6 months
- **Efficacy**: Superior relapse prevention vs [[Azathioprine]] or [[Methotrexate]]
- **Duration**: 2-4 years of fixed-interval infusions based on individual risk

## Disease-Specific Maintenance

### GPA/MPA
**Rituximab-based**: Fixed-interval 500 mg every 6 months
- Can combine with low-dose azathioprine if additional ISA desired
- Glucocorticoids: Taper to discontinuation by month 6-12

**Optional**: Low-dose [[Mycophenolate mofetil]] as adjunct

### EGPA
**[[Mepolizumab]]-based**: Monthly or biweekly IL-5 inhibition
- Permits glucocorticoid reduction/discontinuation
- Often becomes sole maintenance agent
- Sustains benefit beyond 1 year

**Alternative**: Rituximab if severe or refractory to mepolizumab

## Glucocorticoid-Sparing Goals

Modern maintenance aims to achieve:
1. **Rapid steroid taper** → discontinuation within 6 months
2. **Relapse prevention** without prolonged GC exposure
3. **Reduced cumulative toxicity** (infections, bone loss, cardiovascular risk)

## Monitoring During Maintenance

- **ANCA levels**: Baseline and periodic (not sole guide to treatment)
- **Clinical assessment**: Symptoms, organ function, labs
- **Serological monitoring**: Creatinine, urinalysis, proteinuria
- **Infectious surveillance**: Given immunosuppressive burden

## Duration of Maintenance

- **Standard**: 24-48 months of rituximab maintenance
- **Individualization**: Risk factors for relapse may extend duration
- **Discontinuation**: Can consider after sustained remission (discussion of infection vs relapse risk)

## Emerging Questions

1. **When to discontinue**: Can rituximab be safely withdrawn and restarted on relapse?
2. **Duration optimization**: 24 months vs 48 months — which risk groups need longer?
3. **Combination strategies**: Benefit of rituximab + avacopan in maintenance?
4. **Biomarker guidance**: Can ANCA or other biomarkers guide treatment duration?
