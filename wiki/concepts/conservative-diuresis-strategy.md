# Conservative Diuresis Strategy

**Concept:** A diuretic management approach in acute decompensated heart failure that prioritizes preservation of renal function alongside decongestion, accepting slower fluid removal to avoid precipitating irreversible acute kidney injury.

**Context:** Primarily used in patients with advanced chronic kidney disease (eGFR <35 mL/min/1.73m²) or significant acute-on-chronic kidney injury.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Lower-dose loop diuretics** — avoid high-dose infusions; use bolus dosing at moderate doses
2. **Less aggressive urine output targets** — accept >100 mL/hour rather than >150-200 mL/hour
3. **Slower net fluid removal** — prioritize renal stability over rapid decongestion
4. **Dose reduction in response to rising creatinine** — interpret worsening renal function as signal to reduce diuretic intensity, not escalate
5. **Use of aquaretic adjuncts** — add agents like [[tolvaptan]] rather than increasing loop diuretic dose
6. **Daily monitoring** — close tracking of electrolytes, creatinine, urine output

**Rationale:** In high-risk cardiorenal patients, forcing rapid decongestion risks permanent renal injury that outweighs the benefit of faster fluid removal. Dialysis-dependent renal failure is a worse outcome than extended hospital stay for gradual decongestion.

## Evidence from Sources

### Patient B 2026 — ADHF Conservative Diuresis Case

**Patient characteristics:**
- Late-60s female with [[HFrEF]] (EF 28%)
- eGFR 31 mL/min/1.73m² on presentation
- Creatinine 162 µmol/L (baseline 115 µmol/L) — significant acute-on-chronic kidney injury
- BNP 3,100 pg/mL, 6 kg above dry weight

**Conservative strategy implemented:**

| Parameter | Conservative approach | Typical aggressive approach |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------------------------|
| Loop diuretic dose | Furosemide 80 mg IV BD (bolus) | Furosemide 5-10 mg/hour continuous infusion (120-240 mg/day) |
| Urine output target | >100 mL/hour | >150-200 mL/hour |
| Net fluid removal by Day 2 | 2.5 L | 5-8 L |
| Response to rising creatinine | Maintain dose, add tolvaptan | Escalate loop diuretic |
| Length of stay | Day 11 | Day 5-7 |

**Outcome:**
- Creatinine peaked at 174 µmol/L, then improved to 130 µmol/L at discharge
- **Avoided dialysis-dependent renal failure**
- BNP improved from 3,100 → 780 pg/mL
- No 30-day readmission
- Creatinine remained elevated but stable at 30-day follow-up

**Clinical conclusions from treating team:**

> "Conservative diuresis preserves renal function in high-risk CKD patients. Forcing rapid decongestion risks permanent renal injury that outweighs the benefit of faster fluid removal."

> "Worsening renal function during diuresis in this context represents true [[cardiorenal syndrome]] and warrants dose adjustment, not continuation of aggressive diuresis."

> "The primary treatment target in high-risk cardiorenal patients is preservation of renal function alongside decongestion — not decongestion at any cost."

## Contrast with Aggressive Diuresis

Aggressive diuresis strategies prioritize rapid, complete decongestion to minimize length of stay and quickly relieve symptoms. This approach may be appropriate in patients with:
- Normal or near-normal baseline renal function
- No significant AKI on presentation
- Severe pulmonary edema requiring urgent fluid removal
- Lower risk of permanent renal injury

**Trade-off:** Conservative strategy accepts longer hospital stays and slower symptom relief in exchange for renal preservation.

---

## When to Use Conservative Strategy

**Strong indications:**
- eGFR <35 mL/min/1.73m²
- Significant acute-on-chronic kidney injury (creatinine rise >30-50% from baseline)
- History of diuretic-resistant [[acute decompensated heart failure]]
- Previous episodes of dialysis-requiring AKI during diuresis
- Baseline [[chronic kidney disease]] Stage 4-5

**Relative indications:**
- Haemodynamic instability (hypotension)
- Advanced age with frailty
- Multiple comorbidities

---

## Adjunct Therapies

- **[[Tolvaptan]]** — aquaretic that enables free water excretion without further loop diuretic escalation or electrolyte disturbance
- **Strict sodium restriction** — minimize ongoing fluid retention
- **Withholding neurohormonal agents** — temporarily hold [[sacubitril-valsartan]], ACE inhibitors, ARBs during acute phase if hypotension or significant AKI
