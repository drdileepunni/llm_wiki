# ANCA-associated vasculitis—treatment standard

**Citation**: Chalkia A, Jayne D. ANCA-associated vasculitis—treatment standard. PMC, 2024. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11210069/

## Abstract

Comprehensive review of modern AAV treatment based on EULAR 2022 management recommendations. Covers the evolution from cyclophosphamide-based therapy to rituximab-preferred strategies, recent advances in complement inhibition (avacopan) and targeted biologics (mepolizumab), and the shift toward minimizing treatment toxicity while preventing organ damage.

## Key Findings

### Induction Therapy Paradigm
- **Preferred regimen**: Glucocorticoids + rituximab (non-inferior to cyclophosphamide for relapsing GPA/MPA)
- **Cyclophosphamide**: Reserved for severe disease or rituximab contraindications
- **Glucocorticoid tapering**: Rapid reduction (PEXIVAS schedule) reduces infection risk without efficacy loss
- **Plasma exchange**: Beneficial when serum creatinine >300 µmol/L or rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis

### Maintenance Strategies
- **Rituximab maintenance**: 500 mg IV every 6 months for 24-48 months superior to azathioprine/methotrexate
- **Steroid-sparing**: Rituximab maintenance permits glucocorticoid discontinuation within 6 months
- **Immunosuppressive agents**: Azathioprine, mycophenolate mofetil, methotrexate as adjuncts

### Disease-Specific Advances
- **GPA/MPA**: Avacopan (C5a antagonist) superior to standard steroid taper when combined with rituximab/cyclophosphamide
- **EGPA**: Mepolizumab (anti-IL-5) permits glucocorticoid reduction, improves remission, reduces relapse beyond 1 year

### Treatment Principles
- **Urgency**: Delayed remission associates with higher mortality and ESRD risk
- **Rapid initiation**: Prompt diagnosis and treatment critical for preventing irreversible organ damage
- **Toxicity focus**: Long-term management dominated by treatment adverse effects (infections, CVD, cancer) and comorbidities
