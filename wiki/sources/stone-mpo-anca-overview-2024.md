# MPO-ANCA Vasculitis Overview

**Citation**: Stone J. et al., 2024 (extracted from test source)

## Abstract
MPO-ANCA positive disease represents 40-50% of ANCA-associated vasculitis and is characterized by systemic necrotizing vasculitis affecting small vessels. The disease presents with glomerulonephritis as a hallmark feature, often progressing rapidly to ESRD if untreated. Modern treatment emphasizes rituximab-based induction followed by maintenance immunosuppression with azathioprine or mycophenolate mofetil.

## Key Findings

- **Epidemiology**: MPO-ANCA accounts for 40-50% of all ANCA-positive vasculitis cases
- **Clinical presentation**: Glomerulonephritis, constitutional symptoms, arthralgia, pulmonary involvement
- **Renal urgency**: Rapid progression to ESRD without prompt treatment
- **Preferred induction**: Rituximab now first-line (non-inferior to cyclophosphamide per RAVE trial)
- **Maintenance regimen**: Azathioprine or mycophenolate + low-dose corticosteroids
- **Disease distinction**: Pulmonary nodules rare in MPO-AAV (common in GPA)
