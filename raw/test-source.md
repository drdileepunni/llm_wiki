# Test Source: MPO-ANCA Vasculitis Overview

ANCA-associated vasculitis (AAV) is a systemic necrotizing vasculitis that affects small vessels. MPO-ANCA positive disease, also known as microscopic polyangiitis (MPA) or MPO-associated vasculitis, accounts for approximately 40-50% of ANCA-positive cases. The disease is characterized by autoimmune attack against neutrophil cytoplasmic components, particularly myeloperoxidase (MPO).

Clinical manifestations typically include glomerulonephritis, which can rapidly progress to end-stage renal disease if untreated. Patients often present with constitutional symptoms, arthralgia, and pulmonary involvement. Unlike GPA, pulmonary nodules are uncommon in MPO-AAV. The disease requires prompt immunosuppression, typically starting with corticosteroids and either rituximab or cyclophosphamide for induction therapy.

Recent guidelines recommend rituximab as the preferred induction agent for most patients, though cyclophosphamide remains an option for severe disease or those with contraindications to rituximab. Maintenance therapy typically involves azathioprine or mycophenolate mofetil combined with low-dose corticosteroids. The RAVE trial demonstrated non-inferiority of rituximab to cyclophosphamide for induction of remission in AAV.
